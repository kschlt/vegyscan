# Claude AI Instructions - Business Case Documentation

## Context

You are working in the **business case documentation** domain of the VegyScan project. This directory contains strategic market validation and business viability artifacts that form the foundation for product development.

## Directory Purpose

`/docs/business-case/` contains **pre-project strategic groundwork**:
- Market opportunity validation
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Feasibility assessment
- Business model definition
- Go-to-market planning
- Financial viability projections

**This is NOT the same as `/docs/business/`:**
- `/docs/business-case/` = **Strategic layer** (Is there a market? Can we compete? Is it viable?)
- `/docs/business/` = **Product layer** (What should we build? For whom? What features?)

## Core Principles

### 1. Evidence-Based Analysis
- All claims must be supported by data, research, or credible sources
- Cite sources for market data, statistics, and competitive intelligence
- Distinguish between assumptions and validated facts
- Update documents as new data becomes available

### 2. Strategic Alignment
- Business case artifacts inform product decisions in `/docs/business/`
- Market segments identified here should align with personas
- Competitive differentiation should inform product requirements
- Financial projections should align with vision success metrics

### 3. Template-First Approach
- Each artifact includes a research framework section
- Templates guide what data to collect before analysis
- Maintain the template structure even when completed
- Use checklists to track research progress

### 4. Single Source of Truth
- Business case documents are authoritative for strategic business analysis
- Product documents reference (not duplicate) market/competitive insights
- Financial projections here are the source of truth for ROI discussions

## Working with Business Case Artifacts

### Market Analysis
**Focus:** Quantitative market opportunity assessment
- Use TAM/SAM/SOM framework for market sizing
- Include both top-down and bottom-up market estimates
- Identify trends with supporting data (CAGR, growth drivers)
- Segment markets by demographics, psychographics, behavior
- Link market segments to personas in `/docs/business/personas.md`

### Competitor Analysis
**Focus:** Strategic competitive intelligence
- Cover both direct competitors (menu scanning apps) and indirect (manual translation, dietary apps)
- Use structured frameworks (SWOT, competitive matrix)
- Identify white space opportunities for differentiation
- Map competitors to market segments
- Inform product positioning and unique value proposition

### Feasibility Study
**Focus:** Risk assessment across technical, operational, legal, economic dimensions
- Technical feasibility: Can we build this with available technology?
- Operational feasibility: Can we operate/maintain it?
- Legal feasibility: Are there regulatory/compliance issues?
- Economic feasibility: Can we afford to build and run it?
- Timeline feasibility: Can we launch in a reasonable timeframe?

### Business Model
**Focus:** Value creation and capture mechanisms
- Use Business Model Canvas or similar framework
- Define all revenue streams (freemium, subscriptions, ads, partnerships)
- Map cost structure (development, operations, marketing, support)
- Identify key resources, partnerships, and activities
- Define pricing strategy with rationale
- This should align with financial projections

### Go-to-Market Strategy
**Focus:** Customer acquisition and growth plan
- Define target customer segments (link to personas)
- Identify acquisition channels (organic, paid, partnerships, viral)
- Plan launch phases (MVP, beta, public launch, scale)
- Set customer acquisition cost (CAC) targets
- Define activation and retention strategies
- Include content/community strategies if relevant

### Financial Projections
**Focus:** Quantitative business viability model
- Project 1-3 year timeframe
- Include development costs, operational costs, marketing costs
- Model revenue across different scenarios (conservative, realistic, optimistic)
- Calculate key metrics: CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, burn rate, runway
- Determine break-even point
- Calculate ROI and payback period
- Link to success metrics in `/docs/business/vision.md`

## Best Practices

### DO:
‚úÖ Use data and evidence to support all claims
‚úÖ Cite sources with dates (market data becomes stale)
‚úÖ Distinguish between assumptions and validated facts
‚úÖ Update documents when new competitive intelligence emerges
‚úÖ Link market segments to personas
‚úÖ Align financial projections with vision success metrics

### DON'T:

‚ùå Make unsupported claims about market size or competition
‚ùå Ignore indirect competitors or substitute products

## Research Sources

### Market Data
- Industry reports (Statista, Gartner, Forrester)
- Government statistics (census, health departments)
- Academic research papers
- Industry associations
- Surveys and primary research

### Competitive Intelligence
- Competitor websites and app stores
- User reviews and ratings
- Industry news and press releases
- Product Hunt, AppAnnie, SimilarWeb
- Social media and community discussions

### Financial Benchmarks
- Industry financial benchmarks
- Startup metrics (SaaS benchmarks, mobile app economics)
- Public company filings (for comparable businesses)
- VC/accelerator resources (500 Startups, YC, a16z metrics)

## Review and Updates

### When to Update
- Quarterly review of market data and competitive landscape
- When major competitor launches or pivots
- When new market research becomes available
- Before major product decisions or funding discussions
- After completing beta/MVP to validate assumptions

### Review Checklist
- [ ] Are market statistics still current (< 1 year old)?
- [ ] Have new competitors entered the market?
- [ ] Have our assumptions been validated or refuted?
- [ ] Do financial projections align with actual performance?
- [ ] Are go-to-market strategies effective based on data?

## Decision Tracking

When business case analysis leads to strategic decisions:

1. Document the decision in [`/docs/decisions/decision-log.md`](../decisions/decision-log.md)
2. Reference the business case artifact that informed it
3. Update product documents (vision, requirements) accordingly
4. Link back from the business case document to show impact

**Example:**
```markdown
## Decision Impact
This feasibility study led to [Decision LOG-042](../decisions/decision-log.md#LOG-042) to use cloud-based OCR rather than on-device processing due to accuracy requirements and cost constraints.
```
