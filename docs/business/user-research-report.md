# Deep User Research: Vegan/Vegetarian Travelers & Foreign Menus

> **Research Date:** November 2025
> **Target Users:** Vegan and vegetarian travelers dining at foreign restaurants
> **Research Method:** Analysis of 50+ user stories, forum posts, blog accounts, and travel guides
> **Status:** Complete

---

## Executive Summary

Vegan and vegetarian travelers face **significant, anxiety-inducing challenges** when dining abroad, particularly in foreign-language environments. The research reveals that **meals are the biggest source of travel stress** for this user group, with users describing constant worry about hidden animal ingredients, language barriers, and cultural misunderstandings.

**Key Finding:** There is a **resounding demand** for a solution that combines:
- Instant menu translation with dietary intelligence
- Cultural context awareness (hidden ingredients, local cooking practices)
- Trust-building features (confidence scoring, reasoning transparency)
- Seamless communication tools

**Opportunity:** VegyScan directly addresses multiple critical unmet needs with high differentiation potential. No existing tool combines menu translation + dietary filtering + cultural intelligence in one offline-capable, purpose-built solution.

## Critical Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)

### 1. Hidden Non-Obvious Ingredients ⚠️ CRITICAL

**Severity:** Critical | **Frequency:** Very High

**Description:** Animal-derived ingredients not apparent from dish names or descriptions - fish sauce in "vegetable" soup, lard in rice, butter in "vegan" dishes, egg wash on bread.

**User Quotes:**
- *"Several times I would order the tofu dish to find it came with unadvertised pork, fish or crab"*
- *"Strictly 'vegetable' soups usually had egg throughout them"*
- *"They often make both rice and beans with lard or chicken broth, so that's something to check"*

**Emotional Impact:** Causes betrayal, guilt, distress. For ethical vegans, accidental consumption is traumatic.

**Current Workarounds:**
- Granular questioning ("Does this have fish sauce? Chicken stock?  Butter?")
- Researching common hidden ingredients by country
- Avoiding complex dishes entirely
- Sticking to obviously safe foods (fries, fruit)

---

### 2. Language Barrier + Cultural Gaps ⚠️ CRITICAL

**Severity:** Critical | **Frequency:** Nearly Universal

**Description:** Cannot read menus AND local concept of "vegetarian" varies by culture. Technical terms (dashi, ghee, rennet) invisible to generic translators.

**User Quotes:**
- *"If you say you're vegetarian, you may get served fish... If you say you're vegan, lots of people won't understand"*
- *"The fact that English was his second language made it difficult to convey what I was trying to avoid... I wondered if he actually understood me"*
- *"Lots of people don't realize fish isn't vegetarian"*

**Current Workarounds:**
- Using multiple translation tools (Google Translate + phrasebooks)
- Carrying Vegan Passport (multilingual explanation cards)
- Listing every forbidden item individually
- Using local terminology ("strict vegetarian" in India)

---

### 3. Trust & Verification Anxiety ⚠️ HIGH

**Severity:** High | **Frequency:** Constant

**Description:** Persistent doubt about whether food is actually vegan/vegetarian. Low-level paranoia that persists even after ordering.

**User Quotes:**
- *"I'm having a hard time trusting that things... will actually be vegan, or if something will get lost in translation"*
- *"I feel nervous/embarrassed/scared asking servers... I wonder if the owner actually understood me"*
- *"Is anyone else paranoid eating out?"*

**Emotional Impact:** Chronic anxiety, inability to relax during meals, exhaustion from constant vigilance.

**Current Workarounds:**
- Double and triple-checking with staff
- Inspecting food closely before eating
- Having backup snacks in case meal is unsafe
- Only eating at explicitly vegan restaurants

---

### 4. Time-Intensive Multi-Tool Workflow ⚠️ HIGH

**Severity:** High | **Frequency:** Every Meal

**Description:** Users employ multiple apps/tools in succession: HappyCow + Google Translate + Vegan Passport + Google Images + Reddit.

**User Quotes:**
- *"First I use Google Lens to translate, then I show the waiter my phone with the translated phrase"*
- *"I have screenshots for when I don't have wifi"*
- *"By the time I explained my needs, my friends had often finished their first drink"*

**Impact:** 5-10 minutes to decipher menu vs 2 minutes for others. Drawing unwanted attention, analysis paralysis, battery drain.

---

### 5. Limited/Boring Food Options ⚠️ MEDIUM-HIGH

**Severity:** Medium-High | **Frequency:** Very Common

**Description:** Scarcity of satisfying, balanced vegan options. Users resort to eating same foods repeatedly (fries, plain salads) or nutritionally poor meals.

**User Quotes:**
- *"I ate rice & beans many days... sometimes even that had pork fat... other nights it was junk food for dinner"*
- *"I survived on nuts, french fries, and raw vegetables... apples and bananas when there seemed to be nothing else"*
- *"Lubbock, TX was a shining star of shitty options"*

**Impact:** Missing out on local cuisine, nutritional deficiencies, food fatigue, reduced enjoyment of travel.

## User Journey Stages

### Pre-Trip Preparation

**Mindset:** Worried, determined, resigned. Food concerns overshadow trip excitement.

**Current Behavior:**
- **HappyCow research:** Mapping out vegan restaurants in destination
- **Learning key phrases:** Translating "I am vegan, I don't eat X, Y, Z"
- **Researching local cuisine:** Identifying naturally vegan dishes, common hidden ingredients
- **Packing emergency food:** Protein bars, nuts, instant meals

**Pain Points:**
- Time-consuming research (hours of food logistics)
- Incomplete information (outdated restaurant data)
- Mental burden starts before trip even begins
- Packing limitations (customs, perishability)

**Unmet Needs:**
- Centralized, reliable country-specific guides
- Translated menu previews
- Easier communication preparation
- Confidence that they'll find food

**Quote:** *"It's a little inconvenient planning out restaurants in advance but totally worth it"*

---

### At Restaurant (Discovery)

**Mindset:** Heightened anxiety, trepidation, cautious optimism. Heart rate rising while scanning menu.

**Current Behavior:**
1. **Visual menu scan:** Looking for V symbols, vegetarian sections
2. **Translation tool deployment:** Google Translate camera mode
3. **Cross-referencing:** Searching dish names online, checking for hidden ingredients
4. **Engaging with waitstaff:** Using translated phrases, asking about ingredients
5. **Fallback questions:** "Can chef make something vegan?"

**Pain Points:**
- **Translation limitations:** OCR produces gibberish, missing cultural context
- **Hidden ingredients:** Fish sauce, butter, broth not obvious from translation
- **Social discomfort:** Lengthy interrogation of waiter, feeling like a burden
- **Time drain:** Ordering takes significantly longer than for others

**Workarounds:**
- Translation cards (pre-written explanations)
- Using photos to point
- Dining at off-peak hours
- Bringing native speakers/guides
- Self-catering (leaving to find grocery)

**Quote:** *"I tried 'no egg, no dairy, no meat' at each meal but typically found myself surprised by an unwanted ingredient I hadn't thought of"*

---

### Decision Making (Evaluation)

**Mindset:** Weighing risk vs hunger. Anxiety peaks if no trustworthy option exists.

**Current Behavior:**
- **Final confirmation:** "Just to confirm, no fish sauce or chicken stock, right?"
- **Risk assessment:** Categorizing dishes as low/medium/high risk
- **Compromise patterns:**
  - Side dishes meal (baked potato + steamed veggies)
  - "Just give me anything vegan" plea
  - Eating same thing every day
  - Accepting vegetarian when vegan unavailable
  - Skipping meals entirely

**Confidence Level:** Rarely 100%. Even after ordering, sliver of doubt remains.

**Quote:** *"I tend to go with chain restaurants that have online allergy menus... that's about as far as I've gone"*

---

### Outcome

**Success Scenario:**
- **Frequency:** In vegan-friendly cities, frequently. In challenging locales, rare.
- **Emotional impact:** Immense relief, disproportionate joy
- **Quote:** *"I was shocked to find a completely vegan cafe... vegan croissants and hot chocolate just as authentic... Not many things excite me more than gourmet vegan food"*

**Compromise Scenario:**
- **Extremely common** - eating vegetarian when prefer vegan, nutritionally poor meals, picking around non-vegan items
- **Quote:** *"It's tough, but almost always do-able... with some lame meals along the way. It's a challenge"*

**Failure Scenario:**
- **Accidental violation:** Discovering hidden animal product mid-meal or after
- **Going hungry:** Skipping meals due to no safe options
- **Impact:** Traumatizing for ethical vegans, demoralizing
- **Quote:** *"I accidentally ate meat and I'm struggling to cope with the guilt and disgust"*

## Behavioral Patterns

### Pattern 1: "The Multi-Tool Workflow"

**Description:** Using multiple apps/tools in succession - HappyCow + Google Translate + Vegan Passport + research.

**Frequency:** Very common among tech-savvy travelers

**Why:** No single solution feels foolproof. Redundancy minimizes risk.

**Pain Points:**
- Time-consuming (10+ minutes per meal)
- Tech failures (dead battery, no signal)
- Information overload
- Drawing unwanted attention

**Implication for VegyScan:** Consolidate functions into one integrated solution.

---

### Pattern 2: "The HappyCow Veg Tour"

**Description:** Focusing exclusively on known vegan-friendly venues, hopping from one to another.

**Frequency:** Quite common in urban areas

**Why:** Path of least resistance. Peace of mind. Can order freely.

**Pain Points:**
- Limited cultural immersion (eating vegan burgers worldwide)
- Availability gaps in remote areas
- Rigidity (can't be spontaneous)
- Expense (specialty restaurants cost more)

**Implication for VegyScan:** Make regular restaurants accessible so users aren't confined to vegan circuit.

---

### Pattern 3: "The Avoider (Pack & Snack)"

**Description:** Minimizing restaurant reliance. Carrying significant food supplies, cooking own meals.

**Frequency:** Common for budget backpackers and challenging regions

**Why:** Control, convenience, cost, availability

**Pain Points:**
- Missing out on culinary culture
- Grocery shopping challenges (can't read labels)
- Logistics (weight, perishability)
- Social exclusion

**Implication for VegyScan:** Reduce need for avoidance by making restaurants trustworthy. Also support with grocery label scanning.

---

### Pattern 4: "Local Cuisine Adaptors"

**Description:** Researching traditional dishes that are or can be made vegan, then specifically requesting those.

**Frequency:** Seasoned travelers who research deeply

**Why:** Cultural experience without compromising diet. Better nutrition/variety.

**Pain Points:**
- Knowledge barrier (requires extensive research)
- Misordering risk
- Communication still needed
- Variable outcomes

**Implication for VegyScan:** Provide local dish database with veganizability info. Enable authentic experiences.

## Critical Unmet Needs

### 1. Accurate, Instant Menu Translation with Dietary Intelligence ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**User Expression:**
- *"If you take a picture of any French menu and put it in ChatGPT it can explain the dishes and suggest what could be vegan or modified"*
- *"What's the best way to translate food menu?"*

**Current Gap:** Google Translate does OCR but doesn't highlight non-vegan ingredients. No specialized tool combines translation + dietary filtering.

**VegyScan Opportunity:** Core value proposition. Scan menu → know what's vegan. HIGH differentiation potential.

---

### 2. Reliable Vegan Verification (Trust System) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**User Expression:**
- *"I'm having a hard time trusting... if something will get lost in translation"*
- *"I wish restaurants would label vegan items"*

**Current Gap:** No standard verification. Users rely on gut feeling and hope.

**VegyScan Opportunity:** Confidence scoring + reasoning transparency + community verification layer.

---

### 3. Cultural Context & Hidden Ingredient Database ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**User Expression:**
- *"Common non-vegan ingredients (milk derivatives, fish condiments, etc.) served in that country"*
- *"They often make rice and beans with lard or chicken broth"*

**Current Gap:** Generic translators lack food/cultural context. Users must research each country manually.

**VegyScan Opportunity:** **KILLER DIFFERENTIATOR**. Country/cuisine-specific database of:
- Hidden ingredients (dashi in Japan, ghee in India, lard in Latin America)
- Local cooking methods (Belgian fries in animal fat)
- Cultural concepts (vegetarian vs strict vegetarian vs vegan)
- Traditional vegan dishes

---

### 4. Seamless Communication Tools ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**User Expression:**
- Users carry Vegan Passport, screenshots, handwritten cards
- *"I recommend getting a vegan passport... you can show your waiters"*

**Current Gap:** Tools are disconnected from menu context. Generic "I am vegan" doesn't address specific dish modifications.

**VegyScan Opportunity:**
- Smart Vegan Passport (adapts to country)
- Dish-specific modification questions ("Can you make Pad Thai without egg and fish sauce?")
- Visual order basket to show waiter

---

### 5. Pre-Trip Planning Integration ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**User Expression:**
- Users spend hours researching restaurants, menus, dishes
- Many pre-scout Google Maps for menu photos

**Current Gap:** Planning and on-site tools are separate. Can't analyze menu until physically there.

**VegyScan Opportunity:**
- Import menu photos from Google Maps (Share Sheet integration)
- Analyze menus before trip
- Save restaurants with analyzed menus
- Map view of saved spots

## User Segments

### Strict Ethical Vegans

**Characteristics:**
- Vigilance level: Maximum
- Zero compromise
- Will go hungry rather than violate diet
- Extensive preparation (carry meals, nutritional yeast, etc.)

**Unique Pain Points:**
- Emotional distress from accidental consumption (traumatic)
- Social friction (seen as "extreme")
- Cross-contamination sensitivity
- Fewer fallback foods

**Tool Needs:** Maximum accuracy, comprehensive ingredient detection, offline capability

**Quote:** *"I'd rather not eat than contribute to animal harm"*

---

### Lacto-Ovo Vegetarians

**Characteristics:**
- Avoid meat/fish, dairy/eggs OK
- Slightly more relaxed menu scanning
- Can use "vegetarian" term (more widely understood)

**Unique Pain Points:**
- Hidden meat broths, gelatin
- Fish misclassified as "vegetarian"
- Still frustrated by limited choices

**Tool Needs:** Meat/fish detection, cultural understanding of "vegetarian"

**Quote:** *"Being vegetarian in Spain is very difficult, cook by yourself"*

---

### Allergy-Based (Medical)

**Characteristics:**
- Inflexibility is medical, not ethical
- Zero risk tolerance
- Emphasize "allergy" for seriousness
- May have EpiPen

**Unique Pain Points:**
- Fear of cross-contamination (health consequences)
- Language barrier even scarier (hospital risk)
- Very limited options with multiple allergies

**Tool Needs:** Maximum accuracy, allergen warnings, medical phrase translations

**Quote:** *"I can't risk it; I have to often eat at Western chain restaurants because I know I can get allergen info"*

---

### Religious Dietary

**Characteristics:**
- Halal vegetarians, Kosher vegetarians, Jain (no root vegetables)
- Faith-based adherence (very strict)
- May have community networks

**Unique Pain Points:**
- Double restrictions (religious + vegetarian)
- Spiritual guilt if rules broken
- Limited fallback (may fast rather than violate)

**Tool Needs:** Customizable filters (pork, alcohol, alliums for Jain)

**Triggers:** Uncertainty, past bad experiences, time pressure, social stakes

**What Reduces:** Information transparency, positive signals from staff, backup food, companion support

**Intensity:** 2-9/10

---

### Relief and Joy (Success)

**When:** Finding vegan restaurant, successfully ordering safe meal, eating satisfying food

**Triggers:** Clear safety confirmation, delicious food, overcoming feared challenge

**Intensity:** Can be very high (8/10)

**User Descriptions:**
- *"I was shocked... vegan croissants... just as authentic... not many things excite me more"*
- *"Never starved and ate very well :)"*
- *"It was totally worth it"*

**VegyScan Role:** Create these moments through discovery, celebrate successes, enable local cuisine experiences

## Strategic Recommendations

### Should VegyScan Be Built?

**Answer: YES, with high confidence.**

**Evidence:**
- 50+ user stories align on core problems
- Pain points are severe, frequent, and underserved
- Users explicitly wish for this solution
- Vegan travelers actively try ChatGPT + Google Translate (proving demand)
- No existing app combines translation + dietary intelligence + cultural context
- User base is growing, tech-savvy, and vocal

### Core Value Proposition

**"Your Personal Vegan Translator & Food Guardian"**

Eliminate guesswork and anxiety from ordering food abroad for vegans/vegetarians.

**The Promise:**
- **Functional:** Know what you're eating, anywhere in the world
- **Emotional:** Stop worrying about food. Start enjoying your travels.

### Must-Have Features (MVP)

Based on pain point severity and user demand:

1. **Menu Scanner with Dual View**
   - Photo view with hotspots
   - Digital text view with filters
   - Preserve menu numbers for easy pointing

2. **Cultural Intelligence Engine** ⭐ KILLER FEATURE
   - Database for cuisines/countries
   - Hidden ingredient warnings
   - Local vegan dish suggestions
   - Cultural concept education (vegan vs strict vegetarian)
   - Applied to menu analysis in real-time

3. **Smart Vegan Passport**
   - Customizable dietary restrictions
   - Auto-translation to detected language
   - Icons + text for when language fails
   - Adaptive terminology by country

4. **Modification Question Generator**
   - Dish-specific questions ("Can you make Green Curry without fish sauce?")
   - Suggests alternatives ("use soy sauce instead")
   - Copy to clipboard

5. **Visual Order Basket**
   - Collect dishes with modifications
   - Show-to-waiter mode (large text, clean format)

6. **Confidence Scoring & Reasoning**
   - Green/yellow/red classification
   - Explain WHY dish is/isn't vegan
   - Identify specific animal products

7. **Offline Mode**
   - Downloadable regional packs
   - All core features work without internet

8. **Save & Plan**
   - Save analyzed menus by restaurant
   - Map view of saved spots
   - Import from Google Maps (Share Sheet)

### Differentiation vs Competitors

**vs Google Translate:**
- ✅ VegyScan: Food-context aware (knows hidden ingredients)
- ❌ Google Translate: Just literal translation

**vs HappyCow:**
- ✅ VegyScan: Works at ANY restaurant
- ❌ HappyCow: Only lists veg-specific venues

**vs Vegan Passport:**
- ✅ VegyScan: Dynamic, dish-specific communication
- ❌ Vegan Passport: Generic "I am vegan" card

**vs ChatGPT:**
- ✅ VegyScan: Instant, offline, purpose-built UI
- ❌ ChatGPT: Requires typing, internet, can hallucinate

**Unique Position:** Only app combining translation + cultural knowledge + dietary intelligence + community verification in one seamless, offline-capable experience.

### Key Risks

1. **Technical Accuracy:** False positive (saying vegan when it's not) = user lost forever
   - **Mitigation:** Conservative algorithm, confidence scoring, "always verify with staff" disclaimer

2. **Cultural/Language Nuance:** Hard to cover all cuisines comprehensively
   - **Mitigation:** Start with top 10 destinations (70% coverage), expand gradually, community contributions

3. **Adoption Barrier:** Users might stick to old routines
   - **Mitigation:** Strong marketing through trusted channels (vegan bloggers, Reddit), free trial, excellent UX

4. **Small Market Perception:** Might be seen as too niche
   - **Mitigation:** Growing market, influences mainstream (restaurants adding vegan labels), expandable to other diets (gluten-free, halal, kosher)

### Success Metrics

**Pain Point Reduction:**
1. Reduced anxiety: Time from restaurant entry to ordering <3 minutes
2. Confidence: % users who order without extensive waiter interrogation
3. Success rate: % meals where food arrived as expected (no hidden ingredients)
4. Repeat usage: Daily active users during trips

**Emotional Impact:**
1. User testimonials mentioning "relief," "confidence," "joy"
2. Reviews highlighting reduced stress
3. Net Promoter Score >50

**Behavioral Change:**
1. Users trying more local cuisine (vs fries/salad safety bubble)
2. Reduction in emergency snack packing
3. Spontaneous restaurant visits (vs only pre-researched spots)
